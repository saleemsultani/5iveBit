GDPR Compliance Statement

Introduction

This GDPR & Data Processing Agreement (“GDPR Agreement”) outlines how 5iveBit (“we,” “us,” or “our”) handles personal data on our website and within our application. We are committed to complying with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and other relevant data protection laws.

We do not store any user data, cookies, or personal information, nor do we sell user data to third parties. We do, however, rely on third-party APIs and Large Language Models (LLMs) to provide certain functionalities, such as AI-powered chats or analysis. Any data transmitted via these third-party services may be subject to their own privacy policies and terms.

Scope

This GDPR Agreement applies to all users (“you”) who access or use the 5iveBit platform and its associated services. It defines the parties rights and responsibilities concerning personal data processing, data protection, and privacy. By using our platform, you acknowledge and agree to the data practices described herein.

Responsibilities

Under GDPR, 5iveBit generally acts as a data controller for any personal data you voluntarily provide (for instance, via email). This means we determine the purposes and means of processing that data in line with GDPR principles. Our third-party service providers, including LLM vendors, may act as data processors on our behalf. They are contractually obligated to protect your data and only process it per our instructions.

Legal Basis for Processing

Our lawful basis for processing personal data—should you ever provide it—includes:

  • Consent: Where you have given clear consent for us to process your data.
  • Legitimate Interests: Where processing is necessary for our legitimate business purposes, balanced against your rights and interests.
  • Contractual Necessity: Where processing is required to fulfill or enter into an agreement at your request.

Since we do not store or retain personal data by default, these bases typically apply only if you actively share personal information with us.

Data Retention

5iveBit does not retain any personal data unless you explicitly provide it (for example, in a support request). In such cases, we only retain the information for as long as is strictly necessary to address your query or fulfill a service request. We do not store ongoing records of user activity or cookies. Any transient data processed by our system or third-party APIs is not permanently stored on our servers.

Subprocessors

We partner with trusted third-party providers (“subprocessors”) to deliver certain features, such as AI-driven functionalities. These subprocessors may process data on our behalf to enable smooth operation of the 5iveBit platform. We ensure they adhere to GDPR standards by:

  • Conducting thorough due diligence to confirm their compliance with data protection laws.
  • Entering into data processing agreements that outline strict requirements for safeguarding user data.
  • Ensuring they only process data according to our instructions and implement robust security measures.

International Data Transfers

Our third-party service providers may process data on servers located outside your country of residence. When such transfers occur, we take steps to ensure compliance with GDPR by implementing approved transfer mechanisms (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses) and verifying that appropriate safeguards are in place to protect your rights.

Security Measures

Although we do not store personal data or cookies, we maintain technical and organizational measures to protect any minimal or transient data that may be processed through our platform. This includes securing our servers and regularly reviewing our integrations with third-party providers. Should a data breach occur, we will follow GDPR guidelines to investigate, mitigate, and notify any impacted parties promptly.

Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach involving 5iveBit or our subprocessors, we will promptly assess the situation. If warranted under GDPR, we will notify affected users and relevant supervisory authorities within 72 hours. Notifications will include the nature of the breach, potential risks, and measures taken to address the incident.

Rights of Data Subjects

Under the GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access: Request information regarding any personal data we may hold about you.
  • Rectification: Correct inaccuracies or update your personal data.
  • Erasure (Right to be Forgotten): Request deletion of your personal data under certain conditions.
  • Restriction of Processing: Limit how your data is processed in specific scenarios.
  • Data Portability: Obtain and reuse your personal data across different services.
  • Objection: Object to processing activities based on legitimate interests or direct marketing.

Since we do not collect or store any ongoing user data, these rights primarily apply if you voluntarily submit personal information to us (e.g., via email).

Changes to This Statement

We may update this GDPR Compliance Statement to reflect changes in our practices or to maintain compliance with evolving regulations. Any modifications will be posted on this page, and we encourage you to review this Statement periodically.
